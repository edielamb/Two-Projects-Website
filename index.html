TWO PROJECTS
EDIE LAMB

There is an element of secrecy that connects these projects. Both outcomes hide their own true form.





The evolution of the projects began with research and experimentation on subjects related to the prompt. I created quick draft work and several iterations of any ideas that stood out, exploring different project directions.

I was interested in using graphic design to undermine systems of authority and letting the user choose how accessible their work will be to others. While each project is based on different ideas, both outcomes share similar themes; resistance, encryption, and malicious compliance.

SUBVERSITYPE is an abstract, illegible typeface meant to undermine the illusion of authenticity in institutional messages. It is designed for institutions that speak without caring to be understood. Each glyph is built from an equal number of bars on a grid; bar height varies to create the letterforms.

Image Redaction Manual provides a method of encrypting original artwork or photography. The two encryption methods taught in the manual involve physically rearranging an image to redact the significant details, while also providing instructions on returning the image to its original state.

Although the typeface was designed following a strict ruleset and grid system, consistency does not equal comprehension. This project undermines systematic methods of conveying information, reflecting how institutions provide information but do not prioritize comprehension.

This project provides a solution for a current and future problem: unauthorized AI scraping of original creations. By rendering the original work not original, the work can be shared online and avoid potential not original reproductions by AI.

The specimen uses the typeface to set the OCADU Academic Policies in paragraphs of dense text: hundreds of pages of policies that students are expected to know, yet OCAD doesn't make these documents easy to find, read, and memorize.

The manual contains step-by-step instructions, visuals, diagrams, and footnotes to guide the user in performing the craft. Multiple pocket-sized copies of the original manual were also produced, making the image redaction process easily accessible and serving as not original copies of the project itself.
